Mathematical Proof

If 2^x + 2^y = 2^z, then x=y given that x,y and z are positive integers.


Given :


Only those tiles get merged that add up to form a number that is nth power of 2
Tiles begin from number 2 and x, y and z are greater than 1.

Proof :


Consider two tiles be 2^x & 2^y where x,y>=1. As sum 2^x and 2^y is divisible by 2, so consider z such that
2^x + 2^y = 2^z

and n such that,
n=z-x
2^x(1+2^(y-x))=2^z
(1 + 2^(y-x))=2^(z-x)
(1+ 2^(y-x))=2^n
R.H.S is a power of 2 with n>=1 as power z is sum of powers of x and y so, z-x >= 1.
Therefore, for L.H.S as a power of 2, 2^(y-x) must be odd as 1 is added to it to make it even which can be only possible if y-x=0 as 2^0=1.
Hence, y-x=0
x=y.
Hence, proved.
